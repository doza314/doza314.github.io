Reading List

Books I love and Recommend

The Innovators

Walter Isaacson

A fun, thorough history of the digital revolution. Goes to show that what we usually refer to as "innovation" is, more often than not, the result of collaboration, proliferation of ideas, and merging of the arts and sciences. Walter Isaacson books are awesome!

Steve Jobs

Walter Isaacson

This book is just absolutely amazing. I almost cried at the end of this one. Steve Jobs was not a perfect person by any stretch of the imagination, but I think there is much to be learned from both his failures and his successes. His ability to fuse the arts and sciences and to inspire others with his creative vision were unmatched for his time. "The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do." - Steve Jobs

Outliers

Malcolm Galdwell

This one is a collection of stories and analyses on what drives success and how our cultural backgrounds really do play an important role in shaping who we are and how we operate as individuals. Malcolm Gladwell has a really unique perspective on the world.

"Surely You're Joking Mr. Feynman!"

Richard Feynman

This is a collection of anecdotes from his life. Feynman was the embodiment of goofy, mischievous genius. Hilarious and entertaining.

"What Do You Care What Other People Think?"

Richard Feynman

The sequel to "Surely Your Joking Mr. Feynman". I find it just as entertaining as the first one.

Perfectly Reasonable Deviations From the Beaten Track

Richard Feynman

A collection of letters sent from and recieved by Feynman over the course of his life. Provides more insight about the kind of person he was. Another great Feynman read.

Meditations

Marcus Aurelius

An awesome guide to living the good life. Couldn't recommend this enough. Marcus Aurelius was one noble dude.

Anthem

Ayn Rand

A great story about the importance of individuality and the dangers of collectivism.

Be Here Now

Baba Ram Dass

I heard about this from the Steve Jobs biography. This book is incredible and has forever changed my perception of reality. "You're standing on a bridge watching yourself go by" - Baba Ram Dass
