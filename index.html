Reading List

Books I love & recommend

• "Surely Your Joking Mr. Feynman!"

Richard Feynman

This is a collection of anecdotes from his life. Feynman was the embodiment of goofy, mischievous genius. Hilarious and entertaining.

• "What Do You Care What Other People Think?"

Richard Feynman

A sequel to "Surely Your Joking Mr. Feynman". I find it just as entertaining as the first one.

• The Innovators

Walter Isaacson

A fun, thorough history of the digital revolution. Goes to show that what we usually refer to as "innovation" is, more often than not, the result of collaboration, proliferation of ideas, and merging of the arts and sciences.

• Steve Jobs

Walter Isaacson

This book is just absolutely amazing. I almost cried at the end of this one. Steve Jobs was not a perfect person by any stretch of the imagination, but I think there is much to be learned from both his failures and his successes. His ability to fuse the arts and sciences and to inspire others with his creative vision were unmatched for his time. "The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do." - Steve Jobs

• Outliers

Malcolm Gladwell

A great collection of stories and analyses on what drives success and how our cultural backgrounds really do play an important role in shaping who we are and how we operate as individuals. Malcolm Gladwell has a really unique perspective on the world.

• Meditations

Marcus Aurelius

An awesome guide to living the good life. Couldn't recommend this enough. Marcus Aurelius was one noble dude.

• A Brief History of Time

Stephen Hawking

A science classic that, if I'm being quite honest, I don't fully understand. I should probably re-read this!

• Anthem

Ayn Rand

A great story about the importance of individuality and the dangers of collectivism.

• The Alchemist

Paulo Coelho

Just a great story about self-discovery.

• Be Here Now

Baba Ram Dass

I heard about this from the Steve Jobs biography. Filled with seemingly endless insights. Read at your own risk! This will either shatter your perception of reality, or make you angry and confused. In my personal opinion, if any book could be considered 'too powerful', this is one of them. “To him who has had the experience no explanation is necessary, to him who has not, none is possible.” - Baba Ram Dass
